ACCSC Program Advisory Committee Review

ACCSC Appendix III requires PAC members to review and comment on the following items at each meeting. Please provide your observations on each. Detailed written minutes will be generated from your responses.

ACCSC Appendix III — Required PAC Review Item
A. Program Objectives and Length
Are the stated objectives of the AI Application Developer program appropriate for the credential awarded and the entry-level roles targeted? Is the program length of 800 clock hours (approximately 40 instructional weeks) appropriate — neither too short nor too long — for students to acquire the skills needed for initial employment?
Please provide a response.
ACCSC Appendix III — Required PAC Review Item
B. Curriculum Content
Is the curriculum content — covering AI foundations, prompt engineering, web development, database and backend engineering, APIs and system integration, SaaS application development, AI security, AI visibility/marketing automation, and capstone — comprehensive, current, and appropriate for preparing graduates for entry-level employment? Are there content gaps or areas that should be added, removed, or updated?
Please provide a response.
ACCSC Appendix III — Required PAC Review Item
C. Learning Resources, Equipment, and Facilities
The program uses the following platforms as primary instructional tools: Lovable (AI development), Supabase (backend/database), Anthropic Claude API, GitHub, Vercel (deployment), Stripe (payments — test mode), n8n (automation), HubSpot (CRM), and Sentry (error monitoring). Are these tools and resources appropriate, current, and sufficient for students to acquire the skills described in the program? Do they reflect tools in common occupational use?
Please provide a response.
ACCSC Appendix III — Required PAC Review Item
D. Employment Outcomes and Program Viability
Based on your knowledge of the current employment market for AI application development, do you believe graduates of this program — who complete 800 clock hours of structured instruction and a portfolio of deployed applications — would have viable prospects for obtaining entry-level employment in the field? Are there specific gaps in preparation, credential, or experience that you believe would make placement difficult?
Please provide a response.
ACCSC Appendix III — Distance Education PAC Review (Required)
E. Distance Education Delivery
This is a 100% online program. All instruction is delivered via live synchronous sessions (Microsoft Teams or Zoom) recorded in Canvas LMS. A qualified instructor is present for every scheduled instructional hour. Please comment on whether this delivery model is appropriate for the content and the target student population, and whether you have any concerns about the school's ability to deliver quality instruction and verify student identity and engagement in this environment.
Please provide a response.
ACCSC Section IX — Distance Education Student Services (Required)
F. Student Services and Online Library Resource Availability
Because this is a 100% distance education program, ACCSC requires that all student services be fully accessible to online students. The following services were noted as available remotely to enrolled students: online library resources (accessible 24/7 through Canvas LMS, with live librarian support during business hours); admissions, financial aid, tutoring, and career services (available by phone, email, text, and virtual meeting via Microsoft Teams); and technical support for platform access and software configuration. Please confirm whether, based on your experience, this level of student services and library access is appropriate and sufficient for students enrolled in a fully online technical program of this nature.
